When analysing common failures of all four models that we implemented, there were some common themes that arose. In instances such as song ID 114 and ID 1123 (the IDs being from our dataset we used), all four models incorrectly guessed Pop for Metal songs because the lyrics centered on vulnerability and romance. Lines like "Light me a candle, I’m comin’ home / Then leave it by your side where I belong" (ID 114) and "Heartbreak and romance / It's better being numb if you're cracking at the heart" (ID 1123) were misclassified because terms like "home," "heartbreak," and "romance" are statistically dominated by Pop music in the training data, masking the heavier instrumentation that actually accompanied them.

Furthermore, there were also cases where the opposite happened, all four models guessed Metal for Pop songs. For example this is seen in ID 83472 and ID 60071, where the lyrics relied heavily on aggressive metaphors. Lines like "Take away my prime from the mouth of the lion / Savage jaws are the price we pay" (ID 83472) and "Every spark has an ending like a burning fuse" (ID 60071) tricked the models because words like "lion," "savage," "jaws," and "burning" trigger Metal classifications. The primary challenge here is that frequency-based or keyword-based models take words literally, failing to recognize when an aggressive word is just a metaphor for a pop breakup, or when a soft word is sung over heavy, distorted guitars, because these sounds are not part of our dataset. 

Overall though, all four models unanimously were correct roughly 73\% of the time. For example, a successful Pop classification for song ID 99344 ("head buster off top / grab the chopper and chop / at the top is my spot") demonstrates the models capturing rhythmic, more upbeat language and modern slang typical of mainstream hip-hop/pop crossovers. On the other hand, a successful Metal classification for song ID 67457 ("Remnants strewn upon the ground / Drag the carcass withered and brown / Sockets crushed powder remaining") shows the models correctly identifying the overtly dark, morbid, and gothic vocabulary that is common in the Metal genre. In these cases, the semantic meaning perfectly aligns with the literal definitions of the words, allowing the models to predict the appropriate genre. 

There are various things that could be done to potentially improve model performance. Because lyrics only tell part of the story, utilizing external APIs to include acoustic features such as energy, tempo, acousticness, and the tone of instruments would aid in resolving ambiguities between songs with similar lyrical themes but vastly different musical styles. Finally, extracting secondary text features like the overall emotional sentiment of the song, the amount of repetition, and unique word counts can provide vital structural clues that help differentiate genres beyond just the vocabulary and/or text used.
